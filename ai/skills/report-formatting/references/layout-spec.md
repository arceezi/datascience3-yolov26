# Journal Layout Spec

Use this reference when formatting the scientific paper into the project's `v6` style.

## Core Layout

- page size: `A4`, portrait
- title, adviser line, and abstract: full width
- body from `Introduction` onward: two columns
- moderate gutter between columns
- emulate the broad structure of the local sample journal article:
  - full-width first-page lead section
  - readable two-column article body
  - figure and table neighborhoods widened to preserve readability

## Type Hierarchy

- title: centered, large, bold
- adviser line: centered, smaller than title
- section headings: distinct, bold, journal-like
- subsection headings: smaller but clear
- body text: readable, not compressed
- captions: smaller than body text
- references: slightly smaller than body text but still comfortable to read

## Figures And Tables

- figures should center within their available area
- figures inside the two-column body should be resized to fit without overflow
- table captions and figure captions should remain left-aligned and compact
- wide structural blocks such as tables may be isolated into single-column-width sections if needed for readability
- when a major figure or table has a subsection heading and short lead-in text, widen that entire local neighborhood as one block
- do not shrink figures or tables so aggressively that labels become unreadable in Word or PDF

## Export Intent

- DOCX is the editable collaboration artifact
- PDF is the primary review artifact for journal-like fidelity
